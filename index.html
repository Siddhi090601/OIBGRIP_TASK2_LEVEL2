John Keats

a poet of romantic era

Here's a time line of John keats life:

  • 1795 - John Keats is born on Oct 31, 1795, near London. He is first of five children of Thomas Keats & Frances Jennings Keats.
  • 1802 - Infant brother Edward dies.
  • 1803 - Keats begins studies at small school in Enfield, England
  • 1804 - Father Thomas dies of fractured skull while thrown from horse . John's mother, now a widow with four surviving children, remarries later the same year.
  • 1810 - Mother dies of tuberculosis. She leaves the children in the care of their grandmother. Grandmother signs over care of children to guardian, Richard Abbey.
  • 1811 - Abbey pulls Keats from studies at Enfield & apprentices him to a surgeon in nearby Edmonton.
  • 1815 - After four years as apprentice, Keats begins his medical studies at Guy's Hospital in London. Privately, he has started to write poetry.
  • 1816 - He became serious about poetry & met with poet Leigh Hunt, who encourages him, introduces him to other poets (including Percy Bysshe Shelley) & becomes an important influence on his work.
  • 1816 - Decides to abandon his medical career for good so that he can focus on his poetry.
  • 1817 - Keats first poetry collection, a volume simply entitled Poems is published.
  • 1818 - Finishes Endymion, his first major long poem. The poem begins with the immortal line, "A thing of beauty is a joy forever."
  • 1818 - Beloved Brother Thomas dies of tuberculosis at the age of 19.
  • 1819 - After brother's death, Keats moves in with friend Charles Brown in Hampstead, London.He met & fall in love with neighbour, Fanny Brawne. By end of the year the couple is engaged. A year of ups & downs for Keats as he writes many of his best poems, including the famous Odes, but also battles depression & first symptoms of tuberculosis.
  • 1820 - Final volume of poems such as Lamia, Isabella, The Eve of St Agnes and other are published & met with strong reviews.
  • 1820 - Keats doctor informs him that his lungs will not survive an English winter. Keats bids Fanny Brawne a painful farewell and sails to Italy with his friend, the painter Joseph Severn.
  • 1821 - John Keats dies of tuberculosis on Feb 23, 1821 at the age of 25 in Rome. Percy Bysshe Shelley writes the poem Adonais as an elegy for him.
